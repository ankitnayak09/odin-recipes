Omlette

Description

An omelette or omelet is a dish made from beaten eggs fried with butter or oil in a frying pan. It is quite common for the omelette to be folded around fillings such as cheese, chives, vegetables, mushrooms, meat (often ham or bacon), or some combination of the above. To obtain a fluffy texture, whole eggs or sometimes egg whites only are beaten with a small amount of milk or cream, or even water, the idea being to have "bubbles" of water vapor trapped within the rapidly cooked egg. Some home cooks add baking powder to produce a fluffier omelette; however, this ingredient is sometimes viewed unfavourably by traditionalists.

Ingredients

  • 2 eggs
  • 1/4 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of pepper
  • 1 tablespoon of butter

Steps

  1. Crack the eggs into a bowl
  2. Add salt, and pepper to the bowl
  3. Whisk the mixture together
  4. Heat a non-stick skillet over medium heat
  5. Add butter to the skillet
  6. Once the butter is melted, add the egg mixture to the skillet
  7. Let the eggs cook until the edges start to curl up
  8. Using a spatula, gently push one edge of the egg into the center of the skillet
  9. Repeat with the other edges
  10. Let the eggs cook for a few seconds
  11. Flip the eggs over
  12. Cook for another minute
  13. Slide the omelette out of the skillet and onto a plate
  14. Enjoy!
