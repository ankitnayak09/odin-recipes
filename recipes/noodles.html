Noodles

Description

Noodles are a staple food in many cultures made from unleavened dough which is stretched, extruded, or rolled flat and cut into one of a variety of shapes. While long, thin strips may be the most common, many varieties of noodles are cut into waves, helices, tubes, strings, or shells, or folded over, or cut into other shapes. Noodles are usually cooked in boiling water, sometimes with cooking oil or salt added. They are often pan-fried or deep-fried. Noodles are often served with an accompanying sauce or in a soup. Noodles can be refrigerated for short-term storage or dried and stored for future use.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of Raw Noodles
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 2 Cup of water

Steps

  1. Boil water in a pot
  2. Once the water is boiled, add noodles to the pot
  3. Let the noodles cook for 5 minutes
  4. Strain the water from the noodles
  5. Crack open an egg in the pot
  6. Stir the egg and noodles together
  7. Let it cook for 3 minutes
  8. Once the egg is cooked, turn off the stove
  9. Add salt to taste
  10. Enjoy!
